When we first took ownership of Cabana 112 at [The Abaco Club](https://theabacoclub.com/rentals/cabana-112/), the cabana itself was lovely — the octagonal shape, the high ceilings, the way the sunroom looked out over the first fairway. But the bathroom felt like an afterthought.

It was functional. Clean. Just... flat. Nothing about it matched the quiet luxury of being steps from Winding Bay.

So we renovated it. Here's what we did.

## What we started with

The original bathroom was positioned at the far end of the cabana, behind the semi-enclosed bedroom. That layout is actually smart — it creates a natural separation between the sleeping and living areas, and gives the whole unit a sense of flow.

The issue was the shower itself. A standard fixed head, low pressure, tile that had seen better days. After a long morning on the beach or an afternoon walking the fairways, you want a shower that actually feels like a reward.

## The rainfall shower upgrade

The centerpiece of the renovation was replacing the shower head with an oversized ceiling-mounted rainfall fixture. This sounds simple, but getting it right in a space like Cabana 112 required replumbing a section of the wall and resetting the water pressure to compensate for the increased flow volume.

The result is exactly what we were going for. The water falls straight down rather than at an angle, which means you're actually standing *under* it rather than angling into it. On a hot Bahamian afternoon — when the humidity is sitting somewhere around 85% and you've just walked back from Flipper's — it's the closest thing to standing under a waterfall.

## Tile and finishes

We replaced the original floor tile with a matte stone-look porcelain in a warm greige tone. Nothing dramatic — we wanted it to feel calm and natural, not like a hotel lobby. The walls stayed white, with the grout tightened and freshened.

Towel hooks were moved from a single bar to individual hooks at two heights, which turns out to be one of those small things that makes a real difference when two people are sharing a small bathroom.

## What didn't change

The layout itself stayed the same, and for good reason. The bathroom is compact — that's just the nature of a 570 sq ft cabana — but the footprint is well-proportioned. The sink faces the shower, the toilet is tucked to one side, and there's enough clear floor space to not feel cramped.

We also kept the original medicine cabinet rather than replacing it with a mirror. It's more storage, and in a vacation property, storage always wins.

## How it feels now

Honestly, it punches above its weight. Guests staying in a space this size usually expect a perfectly adequate bathroom. What they get instead is something that feels deliberate — the rainfall shower being the main signal that this was thought about carefully.

If you're considering [renting Cabana 112 at The Abaco Club](https://theabacoclub.com/rentals/cabana-112/), this is what you're walking into. A recently updated bathroom, a full kitchen, and a sunroom that looks directly onto the first fairway. The beach is a short walk. The rest of the club — tennis, pickleball, the golf shop, the bar — is closer than you'd think.

It's a small space done right.
